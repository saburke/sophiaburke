Research Experience
Dissertation Work
University of New Hampshire (2014-2020)

Project Summary

My PhD work centered on sub-arctic peatlands with a particular focus on methane (CH4) emissions from peatland thaw ponds.


Title: Investigating the spatial and temporal scale variability of ebullitive flux from a subarctic thaw pond system

Dissertation Chapters
1. Long term measurements of methane ebullition from thaw ponds Burke et al. (2019); JGR Biogeosciences
2. Using unmanned aerial systems to monitor the change in thaw pond size over five growing seasons: Implications for CH4 ebullitive flux (in prep)
3. Using acoustic techniques to monitor CH4 ebullition in subarctic thaw ponds

My dissertation can be found here




Study Site


My main study site is Stordalen Mire, in northern Sweden, 200 km within the Arctic Circle. I used a combination of manual measurements, remote sensing (UAV imagery collection), and acoustical measurements to compile a seven year data set of ebullitive, or bubbling, measurements from these small ponds collected during the growing season (June - August).











Additional Noteworthy Research Expereince
Fulbright US Graduate Fellowship
University of Waikato, New Zealand (2015)

Project Summary
Title: Effect of drought on CH4 flux from Kopuatai Peat bog, Waikato: aboveground and belowground evidence Field site: Kopuatai Peat Dome, Waikato Region

My Fulbright project expanded upon previous work at this site by Goodrich et al. (2015) which found a strong hysteretic response of CH4 emissions post drought. My project paired aboveground eddy covariance measurements with nearby belowground measurements of porewater CH4 concentration and extracted peat cores used for incubations to see how rates of CH4 production varied at this site during a late-summer drought.
