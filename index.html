A curiosity driven Research Scientist with expertise in Peatland Biogeochemistry

Hello there!

My name is Sophia but my friends call me Sophie.

I use she/her pronouns.


As a peatland biogeochemist I am interested in exploring the influence of peatland areas on the global carbon cycle, in particular how rising global temperatures are impacting the ability of peatlands to hold on to the carbon stored within them. I have extensive experience in monitoring methane (CH4) ebullition (bubbling) from subarctic peatland ponds that form due to permafrost thaw. For my PhD I monitored eight thaw ponds at Stordalen Mire in subarctic Sweden using a combination of manual measurements, acoustic measurements and remote sensing. I have traveled to northern Finland and Canada to conduct fieldwork for research projects that are focused on modeling CH4 emissions across the arctic. I also spent a year in New Zealand on a Fulbright Fellowship studying the effects of drought on CH4 emissions from the Kopuatai Peat Dome in the Waikato Region of the North Island through the use of aboveground eddy covariance measurements and belowground measurements such as porewater sampling and peat incubations.


When I’m not doing science, I love to read novels, hike, telemark ski, cook with my partner John, and sew things!
